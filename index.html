Antarctica

Geography

The Ross ice shelf is the largest ice sheet in Antarctica. It is roughly the size of France. It boasts a nearly 600km wall of ice facing the sea front. Which ranges between 15 and 50 metres tall. Although it is estimated that 90% of the ice mass is below the water’s surface.

Dronning Maud land is roughly 2.7 Million square km territory claimed as dependant territory of Norway. Hjalmar Riiser-Larsen was the first to land on Queen Land, in 1930. The territory was later claimed by Norway in 1939. Although it is now part of the Antarctic Treaty system.

The territory is mostly dominated by the Antarctic ice sheet, although large the ice sheet does give way to mountain ranges which break through the ice. This provides breeding habitat to range of Antarctic birds.

The South Pole is the southern most point of the Earth and situated at the site is the Amundsen-Scott South Pole Station. Which staffed year round since 1956, is a research centre for the area.

The south Pole was first discovered by Roald Amundsen. Narrowly beating the ill-fated Captain Scott expedition by 34 days. Scott’s forward party of four later succumbed to the cold and starvation and perished on their return journey.

Ellsworth Land mostly comprised of Ice, although does have a group of scattered mountains. Known as the Ellsworth Mountains.

The area was named for, and largely traversed by American Explorer Lincoln Ellsworth. But little is known of this region as few expeditions have yet to discover many of its secrets.

Ranulph Fiennes

After Attending the Mons Officer Cadet School, he was granted special permission to join the Royal Scots Greys. Which is where his father served and died during World War two. He later joined the Special Air Service and gained experience as a demolition’s expert. However, he was later dismissed for attempting to demolish a Bridge built for the 1967 film Dr. Dolittle.

Sir Ranulph Fiennes is often touted as the worlds greatest living explorer. He has led expeditions up the White Nile in Africa. Led an Expedition with fellow SAS soldiers around the earth polar axis, known his Trans globe expedition. He then became the first to cross unsupported with fellow explorer Mike Stroud.

Climate Change

Unfortunately, due to our global dependence on fossil fuels and other green house gas emitters. The world is in a state of warming. And Antarctica is one of the fastest warming areas in the world. Summer Temperatures have increased by over 3 degrees between 1970 and 2022.

Since the earth has warmed so much, the Southern Ocean has absorbed around 75 percent of the excess heat caused by humans. Penguin colonies have been observed to have shrunk and even disappear completely from some areas.

This excess heat has also caused a steady decline in the ice sheet. And not only is the ice sheet in decline, but it is declining faster each year. Leaving the future of the frozen continent unclear.
